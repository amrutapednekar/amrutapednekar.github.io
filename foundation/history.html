Leprosy is a chronic infectious disease, mainly affecting the skin. It is curable and treatment provided in the early stages averts disability. It is curable with MDT (multi drug therapy); a belief like leprosy is a divine curse, a punishment of the past sins and results of immoral sexual behavior are still prevalent.

When a person is identified with leprosy in the society, he/she suffers economic and social losses, as well as participation restrictions in the community. Stigma attached to leprosy leads to loss of employment even before manual labour becomes more difficult due to disability that often results from late or no treatment. It also leads to exclusion from society, causing physical and emotional distress.

In India, Each of the village or city has a separate slum for leprosy-affected people, which is isolated from community, Even if you go to any bigger cities you will found at least one slum for leprosy, even in the suburb of Mumbai has this.

This is mainly near by the railway stations or suburban railway stations. The patient is isolated by the community and does not have any earning source. There self-respect becomes low, and finally they have to do the work of collecting metal or plastic scraps or garbage. Many time due to easy money the start making country liquor or selling the drugs or even making small robbery.

The only way to bring them in mainstream was by providing them treatment and rehabilitation.
